Scoring Methodology (v2)

The Composite Score (0–100) is a weighted blend of 10 sub-scores. Each sub-score is independently calculated on a 0–100 scale starting from zero — a coin must earn every point through positive signals. No base inflation.

1. Momentum Score (20%)

Evaluates price trend across 4 timeframes — 1h, 24h, 7d, 30d. Rewards sustained multi-timeframe uptrends and penalizes sharp drops. A coin with all timeframes negative scores 0. Strongest factor — momentum is the most persistent alpha source in crypto markets (adapted from Jegadeesh & Titman).

2. Volume Profile Score (18%)

Volume / Market Cap ratio thresholds: >10% (50pts), >8% (40pts), >5% (30pts), >3% (15pts). Below 1.5% penalizes. Includes volume trend detection — compares last 24h hourly price movement vs prior 24h for surge confirmation.

3. RSI Score (12%)

Daily-timeframe RSI — hourly sparkline data is resampled to daily closes before computing RSI. This gives a meaningful swing-trade signal rather than intraday noise. Oversold (<30) = 90pts, favorable (30–45) = 50–70pts, neutral (45–55) = 30pts, overbought (>75) = 0pts.

4. MACD Score (10%)

Hourly MACD (3,7,3) — computed on 168 hourly candles (7 days). Shorter periods than standard (12,26,9) for faster signal detection on short-term trades. Bullish crossover = 90pts, uptrend = 60pts, bearish crossover = 0pts.

5. BTC Correlation (8%)

Pearson correlation computed on hourly returns, not raw prices. Correlating prices is statistically spurious — two uptrending assets always appear correlated. Returns measure actual co-movement. Low correlation (<0.2) = 100pts (independent alpha), high (>0.8) = 5pts (just follows BTC).

6. Fear & Greed Sentiment (8%) — Market-Cap Scaled

Powered by alternative.me Fear & Greed Index. Scaled by market cap tier — small caps (rank 300+) receive only 40% of the contrarian bonus because they crash 3–5x harder in fear periods. Large caps (top 50) get the full bonus. Extreme Fear = up to 90pts (for large caps), Extreme Greed = 0pts.

7. News Sentiment (5%)

Headlines from CryptoCompare classified by LLM on a -10 to +10 scale, mapped to 0–100. Lowest weight because news sentiment is noisy and often priced in by the time retail sees it.

8. ATH Distance (7%)

Distance below all-time high. Near ATH (<5%) = 0pts (limited upside, reversal risk). 80%+ below ATH = 95pts (deep value). Graduated scale rewards coins with room to run.

9. BTC Dominance (5%)

BTC.D >60% = 10pts (alt pressure). BTC.D <42% = 90pts (alt season signal). Derived from CoinGecko /global.

10. Developer Activity (7%)

GitHub commits (4 weeks), PR contributors, stars from CoinGecko dev data. 100+ commits with 10+ contributors = 100pts. Zero activity = 0pts. Filters zombie coins.

Composite Weighting (10-Factor Model)

Mom(20%) · Vol(18%) · RSI(12%) · MACD(10%) · Corr(8%) · F&G(8%) · News(5%) · ATH(7%) · BTC.D(5%) · Dev(7%)

Confidence % (Independent Metric)

No longer a function of the composite score. Confidence measures indicator agreement — what percentage of independent signals (RSI, MACD, 24h trend, 7d trend, correlation, volume, news) point bullish, multiplied by data quality (how many indicators we could compute). A coin can have a high score but low confidence if its signals disagree.

Market Cap Filter: ₹500B INR

Replaces the old price cap. Price alone is meaningless — a ₹2 coin with 10B supply is more expensive than a ₹5,000 coin with 1M supply. Market cap is the correct measure of size. Only assets with market cap ≤ ₹500B appear as actionable opportunities.
